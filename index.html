Research Interests

  • Medical Imaging: Scaling Annotations, Data Synthesis, Image Construction.
  • Biological Imaging: Computational Pathology, Neuroscience, Connectomics.
  • Computer Vision: Image Segmentation, Video Generation, Multi-Object Tracking.
My primary areas of interest in research are biomedical imaging (medical and biological) and computer vision. Most of our understanding of the world stems from interpreting complex sensory signals, particularly visual information, yet turning this raw data into meaningful knowledge remains a fundamental challenge in artificial intelligence. My research aims to develop visual intelligence that can uncover complex patterns in biomedical data, improving diagnostic precision and potentially leading to better patient outcomes and a deeper understanding of health sciences.
